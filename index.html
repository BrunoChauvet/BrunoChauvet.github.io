Community Bookshelf Playing with AngularFire

Investigate AngularJS and Firebase capabilities. Users can manage a virtual library of books they own by searching available titles from Google Book API and adding them to their collection.

Read more »

Taking advantage of Firebase Simple Login, users are authenticated using a supported public account (Github for instance). Books can be searched from the Google Book API and added to user's collection. The powerful 3-ways-binding offered by AngularFire updates user's collection in real time.

Ultimately, the application would allow users to share their library with their neighbourhood, manage books borrowed by the community, support books review and match users preference to provide recommendations. A nice design would be more than welcome :)

  • Ruby on Rails, AngularJS, Firebase AngularFire, Bootstrap
  • Mar 2014 (1 week)
  • Live demo
  • Project source

Silent Auction UFE Charity Gala

As part of the annual UFE (French Foreign Union) Charity Gala, a silent auction is organised and funds revert to Ronald McDonald House at Westmead

Read more »

The items part of the auctions are displayed on a giant screen with the current highest bid and the total amount raised as well as the remaining time. Screen rotates in order to display the full list of items.

Guests have the possibility to log into the silent auction interface via their mobile and place bids. The main screen is updated in real time as well as the user mobile interface with the status of their bids.

During the 2013 Charity Gala, more than $22,000 have been raised. Only volunteers work at the UFE and the totality of funds raised are is given to Ronald McDonald House at Westmead to help childen and their family.

  • Ruby on Rails, AngularJS, Bootstrap, MySQL, Unicorn, Capistrano, RSpec, Cucumber
  • Nov 2013 (2 months)
  • Live demo
